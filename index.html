üí° Key Insights

Extreme Concentration

The wealthiest 1.6% of adults (those with more than $1 million) control 48.1% of global wealth, demonstrating extreme wealth concentration.

Bottom Half Holds Minimal Wealth

The bottom 40.7% of adults (those with less than $10k) collectively hold only 0.6% of global wealth, highlighting significant inequality.

Rising Middle Class

Historical trends show the middle class ($10k-$100k) growing from 13.4% in 2000 to 34.4% in 2022, with projections reaching 37% by 2027.

Regional Disparities

North America and Europe together account for 72.1% of global wealth but only 42.7% of the global adult population, showing significant regional inequality.
